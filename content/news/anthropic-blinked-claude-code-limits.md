Anthropic just bumped Claude Code's weekly usage limits by 50%, good through July 13, 2026. On its own that sounds like a nice perk. In context, it's the third capacity increase in about five weeks — first a temporary "Claude 2x" off-peak promo in April, then doubled hourly limits with peak-hour restrictions removed in early May, and now this.

Why the sudden generosity? Independent comparisons have Codex using roughly 4x fewer tokens than Claude Code for equivalent tasks, meaning developers get meaningfully more done per dollar on OpenAI's tool. Anthropic loosening the leash three times in five weeks isn't a gift, it's a company watching users drift and reacting in real time.

If you've been on the fence about Claude Code's rate limits, now's a good window to actually push it — the extra headroom runs out July 13 unless it gets extended again.
